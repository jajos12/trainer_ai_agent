AI Agents Training

1. Overview and Key Concepts

AI agents are autonomous entities that perceive their environment and act upon it to achieve speci fic goals. They are distinct from simple programs because of their ability to learn, ada pt, and make decisions in dynamic situations. Key concepts include:

  • Perception: How the agent gathers information from its en vironment (sensors, data streams).
  • Action: How the agent interacts with its environment (manipulating objects, sending commands).
  • Goal: The objective the agent is trying to achieve.
  • Environment: The context in which the agent operates (phy sical world, simulation, digital space).
  • Planning: The process of devising a sequence of actions to reach a goal.
  • Learning: The ability of the agent to improve its performance over time.
  • Reasoning: The ability to draw conclusions and make inferences based on available information.

Recent resear ch highlights the increasing sophistication of AI agents, as demonstrated by their applic ation in diverse fields like solving Partial Differential Equations (PDEs) using LLMs (Co dePDE), tackling complex abstract reasoning tasks (ARC-NCA), and enabling autonomous navi gation for UAVs (Towards Autonomous UAV Visual Object Search).
